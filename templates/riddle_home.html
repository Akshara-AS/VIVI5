    How To Solve A Riddle

    Practicing riddles each day can help simplify thinking, provide better memory retention and improve cognitive capabilities. Even challenging riddles can be solved if you use a few simple techniques that is provided here.

    • Identify Clues
    • Try To Interpret
    • Collect All Main Clues
    • List Out Candidate Answers
    • Relax And Chill

    Read the whole sentence first, line-by-line

    Try identifying words that may hint to any object.Look for any language that could have multiple meanings and guard against your own assumptions.

    For example,
    Paper comes in, and paper goes out, but adding words in between is what I’m all about.Who am I?
    >>Clue 1: Paper
    >>Clue 2: Moves in and out
    >>Clue 3: Words on paper
    Thinking : Ink-> Pen or Typed + Moving Paper
    Answer: Printer

    Keep thinking and try interpreting them simultaneously

    Most riddles deal with very familiar topics. Their difficulty comes in how those topics are described. Riddles often create a pattern of associations in order to direct you to the answer.

    For example, a popular riddle from J.R.R. Tolkien’s The Hobbit goes:
    “Thirty white horses on a red hill,
    First they champ,
    Then they stamp,
    Then they stand still.”
    Thinking :This riddle uses familiar ideas (horses, hills) to figuratively express the answer.
    Answer: Teeth

    Keep Tracking Your Clues

    Once you have your clues divided, you must look at them individually to come up with any possible answers. What you need to know is that whether the clues are realting to each other or any of them is qite misleading.Then work on with the clues that you feel could direct to the answer.

    For example, Why is the letter A like a flower?
    >>Clue 1: Letter 'A'
    >>Clue 2: Shape is flower
    >>Clue 3: Following letter-'B'
    Thinking : Clue(1+3)=A followed by B(bee); Clue 2 =False
    The answer is It is follow by B's.

    List Out The Posiible Answer

    Consider making a list of candidate answers matching one part, and see if it fits with other parts.Look for conflicts if available with the conjectures and adjust them.Try to relate the clues found with the answers you think that may fit in and finally you reach precision.

    For example,
    I wear you down, yet you'll mourn me once I fly. You can kill me, but I will never die. What am I?
    >>Answer 1: Dress
    >>Answer 2: Opportunity
    >>Answer 3: Time
    Thinking : Time fits all the description mentioned
    The answer is Time
