    Your Body Your Wealth

    Regularly check the scale.

    Quality over Quantity

    To be able to eat as much as you want… that is all well and good. But you still need to eat a healthy diet if you want to gain weight fast.

    • When you focus on gaining weight, high calorie foods should be a priority.
    • You should, always eat enough fruit and vegetables to cover your micronutrient requirement.

    Eat Regular Meals

    You don’t have to eat your daily calorie requirement in three main meals.

    • Five or six regular meals (spaced out throughout the day) are easier on your stomach and keep you feeling full.
    • You should avoid skipping meals and you should always have some snacks on you in case you get hungry while on the go.

    Gradually Increase Portion Size

    If you try to start eating enormous meals from one day to the next, it won’t work. Gradually increase your portion size every week instead.

    • This way your body can get used to larger meals slowly.
    • After a few weeks you will be able to eat considerably more. If you add high calorie foods to your meals as well, you will significantly increase your calorie

    Light Products? No Thanks!

    Stay away from products labeled “low-fat” or “light”. At the grocery store, make sure to buy the regular fat version.

    • Also, foods naturally high in fat like avocado, nuts, seeds or high-quality vegetable oil should be a regular part of your meal plan.
    • They fill you up and provide you with plenty of nutrients and calories.

    Track Your Calories

    Keep in mind of everything you eat and drink.

    • Food tracking helps you learn more about your eating habits.
    • Perhaps you can squeeze in a few more calories here or there? Or maybe you forgot to eat breakfast yesterday?

    Rely on Strenght Training

    Exercise is very important for gaining weight. Intense bodyweight training, strengthens your muscles and also stokes your fat-burning engine.

    • Endurance sports like running or biking are also important for your health, but they burn a lot of calories without really stimulating muscle growth.
    • If you want to gain healthy weight, we recommend focusing on strength training (3-4 times a week) with more weight and fewer repetitions. This is the most effective way to experience muscle growth.

    Have an occasional treat

    Even when you're underweight, be mindful of excess sugar and fat. An occasional slice of pie with ice cream is OK.

    • Most treats should be healthy and provide nutrients in addition to calories.
    • Bran muffins, yogurt and granola bars are good choices.

    Eat right before bed

    A lot of our healing, repair, and regeneration takes place while we sleep. It's like rush hour for building muscle and lean tissue, so eating a healthy snack right before bed ensures a fresh supply of nutrients that are available to "go to work" inside the body.

    • A great option that won't leave you feeling stuffed might be a small bowl of salad made with 100 percent whole-grain stuffs
    • You can slso include vinaigrette made with extra-virgin olive oil,or an organic crumbled cheese.
